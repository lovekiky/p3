about

Otl was born in 1922 in Ulm-Söflingen, Germany. As a child, Otl was interested in philosophy, literature, and art. Through his schooling, he became good friends with his classmate Werner Scholl and his siblings.

Around 1937, Otl opposed the Nazi movement and was arrested for refusing to join the Hitler Youth. Though he was later drafted into the German army during WWII, against his political and moral beliefs.
Sadly, In 1943, Werner and some of his siblings were executed for their involvement in the White Rose resistance movement against Nazis which Otl was also a part of. Two years later, he deserted the army and went into hiding at the Scholl’s house.

When the war ended in 1946, he began to study sculpture at the Acadamy of Fine Arts in Munich.

In 1952, Otl married Inge Scholl, the older sister of Werner; one year later, along with friend and Swiss artist and architect Max Bill, the couple opened the Ulm School of design where Otl taught and opened an on-campus design firm.

After the school closed, Otl and his family moved to Allgau, Germany where he bought a property called Rotis and built two studios. Throughout the 70’s and 80’s he worked on various projects for himself and companies.
